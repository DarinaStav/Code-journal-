"How strange: you see people like icebergs - very few from the outside, and the most part is under the water, hidden. And you can see the outside only from one side. Icebergs are said to capsize when the underwater part melts. And this is what happens with people: in war, during disasters. The bottom is often unpleasant. But heroism is not uncommon."

- Mykola Amosov

  • Amosov was the first in the USSR to perform the mitral valve replacement in 1963 and to implement the antithrombotic artificial heart valve in 1965. During the years of his practice Amosov performed over 6 thousand heart surgeries.

  • In 2008, Amosov was recognized as "a great Ukrainian of all time", the second person, after Yaroslav the Wise, to be awarded such a honorary title.

  • In 1963, he was the first in the USSR to perform mitral valve prosthetics, and in 1965, he introduced antithrombotic heart valve prostheses. Over the years of his cardiosurgical practice, Amosov has performed more than 6,000 heart operations.

  • Academician Amosov lived a long and accomplished life. He practiced till the age of 80 and died when he was almost 90.
